IDUKKI



The Green Paradise of Kerala

A paradise for nature enthusiasts and those seeking respite in the lap of lush greenery. Famous for its sprawling tea plantations, serene lakes, and rolling hills, Idukki offers an idyllic retreat. The Idukki Dam, one of Asia's largest arch dams, is a popular attraction, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. Visitors can explore the hill stations of Munnar and Vagamon, revel in the beauty of Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary, or take a houseboat cruise on the tranquil Idukki reservoir. With its rich biodiversity, cool climate, and myriad opportunities for trekking and adventure, Idukki is a haven for travelers looking to immerse themselves in the natural wonders of southern India.



Places to visit




Munnar

A renowned hill station in the Indian state of Kerala, known for its lush tea plantations and stunning natural beauty. The region offers a cool and refreshing climate, making it a popular destination for travelers seeking respite from the heat. Munnar is also famous for its picturesque valleys, pristine lakes, and the opportunity to explore a variety of outdoor activities, including trekking and wildlife encounters in nearby national parks.

Idukki Arch Dam

The Idukki Arch Dam is a remarkable engineering marvel located in the Indian state of Kerala. It stands as one of the tallest arch dams in Asia and spanning the Periyar River. Its sprawling reservoir, Idukki Lake, is not only a source of power but also a captivating scenic attraction, drawing visitors with its serene waters, surrounded by lush hills and dense forests.

Vagamon

This serene hill station , is a hidden gem for nature lovers and is renowned for its lush green meadows, dense pine forests, and rolling hills that create a picturesque landscape. Vagamon offers a pleasant escape from the hustle and bustle of city life, with its cool climate and stunning vistas. Adventure seekers also flock to Vagamon for paragliding and trekking opportunities, making it a versatile destination that appeals to both relaxation and adventure enthusiasts.



Kumily

This charming town is renowned for its lush spice plantations, including cardamom, pepper, and cinnamon, which contribute to the rich spice trade in the region. Kumily is a hub for eco-tourism, offering visitors the chance to explore the serene Periyar Lake, partake in wildlife safaris, and immerse themselves in the natural beauty and cultural diversity of the Western Ghats. The bustling local markets also make Kumily a great place to purchase high-quality spices and other regional products.

Kalvari Mount

Kalvari Mount is a serene and sacred hill offering panoramic views of the nearby landscapes. It is a popular pilgrimage destination with a Christian cross at the summit, attracting devotees and tourists alike. Visitors can enjoy a peaceful atmosphere, lush greenery, and beautiful sunsets from this vantage point. Kalvari Mount provides a tranquil escape and a spiritual experience in the midst of nature.

Anamudi

Anamudi is the highest peak in South India. It is a popular trekking destination for adventure enthusiasts and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding lush landscapes. Anamudi is situated in the Eravikulam National Park, making it a vital ecological area, home to various species of flora and fauna, including the Nilgiri Tahr. It's an iconic and awe-inspiring natural landmark in the region.
