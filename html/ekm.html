ERNAKULAM



The Queen of The Arabian Sea

Enchants travelers with its diverse tapestry of experiences. With its pristine beaches, historic landmarks, and lush landscapes, Ernakulam offers a journey through nature, culture, and tradition. The enchanting backwaters, dotted with traditional houseboats, invite tranquil exploration, while the vibrant streets showcase the region's rich history and culture. Ernakulam is a harmonious blend of tradition and modernity, where ancient temples and colonial-era architecture coexist with a thriving urban landscape. From the stunning sunsets over Fort Kochi's waterfront to the delectable flavors of Kerala cuisine, Ernakulam is a captivating destination for those seeking an unforgettable and authentic travel experience on India's southwestern coast.



Places to visit




Vypeen Island

A beautiful and serene island located off the coast of Kochi. Renowned for its picturesque beaches, including Cherai Beach, which is a popular tourist destination. The island is connected to the mainland by a series of bridges and ferries, providing easy access for travelers. Vypeen Island offers a unique blend of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and a tranquil atmosphere, making it a must-visit destination.

Hill Palace

Hill palace is a historical museum and palace complex.It was the official residence of the Kochi royal family. The palace showcases a vast collection of artifacts, including royal heirlooms, paintings, and sculptures.Visitors can explore the beautifully landscaped gardens and experience the rich heritage of Kerala's royal history at Hill Palace.

Marine Drive

Marine Drive is a picturesque promenade that stretches along the Arabian Sea. This iconic waterfront area offers stunning views of the Kochi harbor, making it a popular destination for both tourists and locals. It's an ideal place for a leisurely walk, with well-maintained walkways and beautiful seating areas. It is is not only a perfect spot to enjoy a serene sunset but also a bustling area with various shopping options, restaurants, and entertainment activities.



Mattancherry

Mattancherry is a historic neighborhood renowned for its rich cultural and architectural heritage, with influences from various cultures and eras. The area is particularly famous for the vibrant and colorful Dutch Palace, which features exquisite murals portraying Hindu mythology. Mattancherry is also known for its spice markets, reflecting its historical role as a center of spice trade during the colonial period.

Fort Kochi

This charming coastal area is famous for its well-preserved colonial architecture, a testament to its Portuguese, Dutch, and British influences. Visitors can explore ancient Chinese fishing nets, centuries-old churches, and vibrant art scenes in Fort Kochi. Additionally, the cultural diversity and rich history make it a popular tourist destination that offers a unique blend of tradition and modernity.

Mangalavanam Bird Sanctuary

A thriving wetland ecosystem that serves as a crucial refuge for various bird species, especially migratory birds during their annual journeys. Visitors can experience the mesmerizing sights and sounds of nature while observing numerous bird species, including both resident and migratory birds, in their natural habitat. It is a must-visit destination for bird enthusiasts and nature lovers.
