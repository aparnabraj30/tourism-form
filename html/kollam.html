KOLLAM



Backwaters and Beyond

Kollam, a coastal paradise on the southwestern tip of India, lures travelers with its unique blend of natural beauty and cultural charm. Its pristine beaches, including the serene Thirumullavaram and the historic Kollam Beach, invite visitors to bask in the golden sands and soak in the mesmerizing sunset views over the Arabian Sea. Kollam is also renowned for its intricate network of backwaters, where traditional houseboats glide serenely through lush greenery, offering a glimpse into the tranquil life of the locals. The town's rich heritage is on full display in its ancient temples, historic churches, and bustling markets, where spices, handicrafts, and seafood tempt the senses. As the gateway to the famed Ashtamudi Lake, often referred to as the 'Gateway to the Backwaters,' Kollam promises an unforgettable experience, where nature and culture converge in perfect harmony.



Places to visit




Jatayu Earth's Center

A unique and ambitious eco-tourism project that captivates visitors with its blend of myth, nature, and adventure. The center boasts a colossal sculpture of Jatayu, one of the largest bird sculptures in the world. Beyond the awe-inspiring statue, visitors can immerse themselves in the natural beauty of the surroundings, with hiking trails, rock-climbing, and zip-lining, all while enjoying panoramic views of the lush landscape.

Munroe Island

A picturesque destination, renowned for its serene backwaters and lush greenery, making it a haven for nature enthusiasts. The island offers an authentic village experience, allowing visitors to explore traditional lifestyles and cultural practices. Boating through its intricate network of canals and enjoying the tranquil environment is a unique and unforgettable experience for travelers seeking a break from the hustle and bustle of city life.

Kollam Beach

Renowned for its natural beauty and serene ambiance. This picturesque beach offers visitors a breathtaking view of the vast ocean. A popular destination for both tourists and locals, who come to enjoy the sandy shores, calming waves, and stunning sunsets. It's an ideal place for relaxation, beachside walks, and water-based activities, making it a must-visit location in Kerala's coastal landscape.



Palaruvi Waterfalls

One of the most picturesque and the stunning waterfalls in the region, surrounded by lush greenery and a tranquil ambiance. Palaruvi is known for its unique horsetail shape, where the water flows down gracefully, resembling the tail of a horse. The waterfall is a popular tourist destination and offers visitors the chance to relax and enjoy the natural beauty of the area.

Thangassery

A historic coastal town with a rich heritage. It is renowned for its ancient churches, including the 18th-century Tangasseri Lighthouse, which offers breathtaking panoramic views of the Arabian Sea. The town also boasts colonial-era remnants, with remnants of Dutch and Portuguese forts, reminding visitors of its storied past. Thangassery's tranquil beaches, such as the Thangassery Beach and the Hawa Beach, are ideal for relaxation and enjoying the coastal beauty of Kerala.

Ashtamudi Lake

Ashtamudi is renowned for its pristine beauty and serene backwaters, where traditional houseboats glide through the labyrinth of canals, flanked by swaying coconut palms and thriving birdlife. This majestic lake is a treasure trove for nature enthusiasts, offering tranquil boat rides, fishing opportunities, and a chance to witness the serene life of local communities along its picturesque shores.
