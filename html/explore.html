Wayanad

Wayand

A must-visit destination for tourists. Trekking enthusiasts can explore the mesmerizing Chembra Peak, and adventure seekers can indulge in river rafting along the Kabini River. Don't miss the serene Pookode Lake and the historic Edakkal Caves. You'll find a perfect blend of nature, adventure, and culture, making it a captivating destination for all types of travelers.

Pathanamthitta

Pathanamthitta

A district brimming with natural beauty and spiritual significance and is home to the renowned Sabarimala Temple. The annual Padayani festivals provides visitors with an opportunity to immerse themselves in Kerala's rich cultural heritage. Moreover, the district is blessed with places like Konni and Gavi, an eco-tourism spot offering thrilling adventures and jungle safaris.

Kollam

Kollam

Often referred to as the "Gateway to Kerala's Backwaters," is a captivating district renowned for its scenic backwaters, cultural heritage, and unique experiences. The crown jewel of Kollam's tourism is the stunning Ashtamudi Lake, where visitors can embark on serene boat rides and houseboat cruises, immersing themselves in the tranquility of the waterways.

Palakkad

Palakkad

In Palakkad, you will find a diverse range of attractions that will captivate you. The Malampuzha Dam and its beautiful gardens provide an ideal setting for a relaxing day outdoors. You can also explore the rich cultural heritage, particularly through the centuries-old Kalpathy Ratholsavam. A visit to the Palakkad Fort takes you back in time, allowing you to immerse yourself in historical charm. The district's unique blend of natural beauty and cultural richness ensures you'll have a memorable experience.

Idukki

Idukki

A paradise for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers. This picturesque district offers breathtaking hill stations, like Munnar, where lush tea plantations and cool climate create an idyllic retreat. For those seeking serenity, the calm waters of Idukki's reservoirs and the tranquil atmosphere in places like Thekkady make it an ideal destination to unwind amidst nature's beauty.

Ernakulam

Ernakulam

This bustling district is highlighted by the vibrant city of Kochi, where historic charm and modern amenities coexist harmoniously. You can explore the iconic Fort Kochi with its colonial-era architecture, Chinese fishing nets, and quaint streets. The district's maritime significance is evident at the Marine Drive, where one can witness stunning sunsets over the Arabian Sea.

Thiruvananthapuram

Thiruvananthapuram

You'll find a treasure trove of tourist attractions that promise an unforgettable experience. The city's stunning beaches, such as Kovalam and Varkala, beckon you to soak up the sun and relax by the Arabian Sea. Explore the grandeur of the Padmanabhaswamy Temple, an architectural marvel steeped in history. Don't miss the chance to witness vibrant festivals, where you can partake in local traditions and enjoy the lively atmosphere.

Kozhikode

Kozhikode

As you explore the historic trading ports along the Malabar Coast, you'll be transported back in time to the era of Vasco da Gama. The city's rich culinary heritage will tantalize your taste buds, with the delectable Malabar cuisine awaiting you at every corner. You'll also discover serene beaches like Kappad, inviting you to unwind and appreciate the tranquil Arabian Sea. Kozhikode ensures that you immerse yourself in a journey that will leave an indelible mark on your travel memories.

Alappuzha

Alappuzha

The primary attraction is the enchanting backwaters, a network of serene canals and waterways that can be explored by houseboat cruises, providing a picturesque view of the countryside and allowing travelers to unwind in the lap of nature. You can immerse themselves in the local culture by attending vibrant art forms and exploring historical sites, like the Krishnapuram Palace, which houses a museum with a remarkable sculpture of the Gajendra Moksha

Kasargod

Kasargod

Kasaragod offers a unique blend of cultural heritage and untouched natural beauty that can captivate you. With a distinct cultural influence from both Kerala and Karnataka, you can explore historic fortresses, like Bekal Fort. Whether you're a nature enthusiast, history buff, or cultural explorer, Kasaragod has something special to offer, making it a hidden gem for those seeking an authentic Kerala experience.

Thrissur

Thrissur

In Thrissur, you'll experience a unique blend of tradition and festivity. The renowned Thrissur Pooram, is a visual and auditory spectacle you won't want to miss. The city's history is well-preserved in its ancient temple, the Vadakkunnathan Temple, and you'll find exquisite examples of local architecture. Stroll through the bustling markets and sample the delicious local cuisine. You'll also enjoy exploring the nearby beach towns and their serene, unspoiled beauty.

Kottayam

Kottayam

Kottayam welcomes you to explore the serene Vembanad Lake, where you can embark on houseboat cruises. Besides its natural beauty, it's rich religious diversity invites you to visit renowned churches and temples, with the tranquil ambiance adding to the spiritual journey. Don't forget to visit Ambattu Paadam, a picturesque spot offering breathtaking views of water lilies. Here, you can engage with the friendly community, and make unforgettable memories in the heart of this charming district.

Malappuram

Malappuram

Known as the "Land of Hills," is a diverse district in Kerala that offers you a unique blend of natural beauty, rich Islamic heritage, and cultural diversity. As you explore the region, you'll be captivated by lush forests, rolling hills, and tranquil rivers. The district's numerous temples, mosques, and educational institutions reflect its cultural richness. It's cuisine is a delightful fusion of flavors, and you can savor local delicacies as you immerse yourself in its vibrant markets. With its harmonious coexistence of history and modernity, Malappuram invites you to experience an enriching and culturally diverse journey.

Kannur

Kannur

A treasure trove of culture and natural beauty waiting for you to explore. It is famous for its unique theyyam rituals, where you can witness vivid and mystic performances that are an integral part of the region's tradition. Enjoy the unspoiled tranquility of Payyambalam Beach, where you can relax and take in the picturesque sunset views. Kannur's thriving handloom industry offers you an opportunity to shop for traditional fabrics and crafts. Immerse yourself in the local culture and traditions as you visit historic forts, art centers, and heritage sites, making your trip to Kannur a delightful blend of art, heritage, and serene coastal experiences.
