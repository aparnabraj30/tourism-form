WAYANAD



Enchanting Tapestry of Emerald Forests

Nestled in the Western Ghats, Wayanad boasts lush green forests, pristine wildlife sanctuaries, and picturesque landscapes. This region is known for its vibrant flora and fauna, including the majestic Indian elephants. Wayanad is also rich in tribal culture and heritage, offering visitors a unique opportunity to explore the traditions of indigenous communities. The district's serene lakes, cascading waterfalls, and tranquil plantations of tea, coffee, and spices add to its charm. With its pleasant climate and a myriad of outdoor activities, Wayanad is a perfect destination for those seeking an escape into the lap of nature and culture.



Places to visit




Banasura Sagar Dam

The largest earthen dam in the country and the second-largest in Asia. The dam's vast reservoir, encircled by lush hills and verdant forests, creates a picturesque backdrop for boating and other water-based activities. Adventurers are drawn to its surroundings for trekking, as Banasura Hill, part of the Western Ghats, is a hiker's paradise.

Edakkal Caves

Edakkal Caves, situated in the Wayanad district of Kerala, India, are a geological and historical wonder that entices both nature enthusiasts and archaeology aficionados. These fascinating caves are renowned for their unique rock engravings and ancient petroglyphs that date back thousands of years, offering a remarkable window into prehistoric human civilization.

Kuruva Island

This uninhabited paradise is crisscrossed by the gentle flow of the Kabini River, and it beckons visitors with its lush, densely forested landscape. The island is a sanctuary for a rich diversity of flora and fauna, with a pristine ambiance that offers solace to those who seek respite from the bustling world.

Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary

A haven for biodiversity enthusiasts and nature lovers. Trekking through the dense canopy of trees and exploring the pristine wilderness, visitors can immerse themselves in the natural beauty and serenity that Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary offers, making it a must-visit destination for those seeking a glimpse of India's untamed wilderness.

Neelimala View Point

a breathtaking panorama of verdant valleys and rolling hills. The vantage point is a treasure trove of natural beauty, with the lush forests and mist-clad mountains creating a picturesque backdrop. Hiking up to this scenic spot unveils a mesmerizing vista, making it a must-visit for nature enthusiasts and photographers seeking a glimpse of Kerala's pristine landscape.

Bamboo Forest

The towering bamboo canes form a natural canopy, casting dappled shadows on the forest floor. The rustling of bamboo leaves in the gentle breeze creates a soothing, melodic backdrop to the symphony of chirping birds and buzzing insects. Exploring the serene pathways that wind through the Wayanad bamboo forests is an immersive experience, allowing one to connect with the untamed beauty of nature in this picturesque region.
